Email Abuse Intelligence · Pillar BEC

Business Email
Compromise.

The costliest email abuse of all carries no malware to catch — just a trusted identity, a plausible request, and money that moves. This pillar models how BEC is built, from target research to cash-out. The third pillar of Email Abuse Intelligence.

Namespace EAI · BEC Release v1.0 Posture Defensive & analytical Techniques 27
01 · Pillar thesis

Nothing to detonate. Everything to verify.

Business Email Compromise defeats the tools built for spam and malware because it carries neither. There is no attachment to sandbox and often no link to follow — only a message that looks exactly like a routine request from someone you trust. The payload is social, and the vulnerability is process.

That is why BEC belongs in an intelligence model, not a filter. It is a chain — research, access, reconnaissance, engagement, execution and cash-out — and it is broken not by a signature but by verification designed into the payment process. This pillar names each step so defenders can place controls where they matter, and it bridges to the phishing pillar, the usual door through which BEC access begins.

02 · Scope & boundaries

Defensive by definition.

What this is
  • A classification and reference model for understanding BEC fraud.
  • Analytical and defensive — for finance teams, defenders and responders.
  • Focused on observable signals and process controls.
  • Open and citable, with stable identifiers.
What this is not
  • Not a script, template or toolkit for committing fraud.
  • Not operational instructions for impersonation or payment diversion.
  • Not a directory of targets, vendors or accounts.
  • Not a substitute for lawful, authorised testing and controls.

Every technique is described at the level of classification and defence — what it is, how it is observed, and how it is countered. The emphasis throughout is on the process controls and verification that stop the fraud.

BEC-RT

Target Selection & Research

How the right people, relationships and moments are chosen.

BEC-RT-01

Finance-role identification

What it is
Staff able to move money — accounts payable, finance, executives — are pinpointed.
Observable signals
Targeting or impersonation focused on payment-authorised roles.
Defensive response
Extra controls and training for anyone who can authorise or execute payments.
BEC-RT-02

Relationship mapping

What it is
Trust relationships (executive-to-assistant, company-to-vendor) are mapped to make requests credible.
Observable signals
Impersonation exploiting one specific, known relationship.
Defensive response
Codified verification norms for relationship-based payment requests.
BEC-RT-03

Transaction-timing research

What it is
Requests are aligned to genuinely expected payments or deals.
Observable signals
A request coinciding suspiciously with a real, pending transaction.
Defensive response
Dual control and out-of-band checks on every payment and change.
BEC-RT-04

Financial-calendar awareness

What it is
Month-end, quarter-end, payroll and tax windows are chosen for pressure.
Observable signals
Pressure timed precisely to a financial deadline.
Defensive response
Heightened scrutiny during known high-risk financial windows.
BEC-AC

Access & Positioning

How the sender obtains a credible position to send from.

BEC-AC-01

Internal account compromise

What it is
An internal mailbox is taken over to send from a genuine identity (bridges PHI-CC-02).
Observable signals
Anomalous login or behaviour on a finance or executive account.
Defensive response
Phishing-resistant MFA, conditional access, session monitoring.
BEC-AC-02

Vendor email compromise (VEC)

What it is
A supplier's real mailbox is compromised and used against its customers.
Observable signals
A banking-change request from an otherwise-genuine vendor account.
Defensive response
Verify vendor changes out-of-band via a pre-known contact.
BEC-AC-03

Lookalike domain

What it is
A cousin domain of the company or vendor is registered (bridges SPM-ID-02).
Observable signals
A near-miss domain in the from or reply-to address.
Defensive response
Lookalike monitoring and external-sender banners.
BEC-AC-04

Display-name impersonation

What it is
Only the friendly name of an executive or vendor is forged (bridges SPM-ID-03).
Observable signals
A known name paired with an unfamiliar address.
Defensive response
Header-from analysis and first-contact flags.
BEC-AC-05

Mailbox-rule persistence

What it is
Inbox rules hide replies and alerts on a compromised account (bridges PHI-OB-03).
Observable signals
New auto-forward, move or delete rules.
Defensive response
Audit inbox rules and alert on rule creation.
BEC-RE

Mailbox Reconnaissance

How context is gathered to make the fraud seamless.

BEC-RE-01

Thread-context harvesting

What it is
Existing threads are read to mimic tone and reference real context.
Observable signals
Messages citing accurate, non-public internal detail.
Defensive response
Treat anomalies as possible compromise; segment sensitive threads.
BEC-RE-02

Pending-payment discovery

What it is
Invoices and payments in flight are located so a real one can be hijacked.
Observable signals
A genuine invoice quietly redirected or altered.
Defensive response
Verify banking details on every payment, every time.
BEC-RE-03

Signature & tone cloning

What it is
Style, signature and disclaimers are replicated for authenticity.
Observable signals
A near-perfect stylistic match with a subtle address change.
Defensive response
Never treat tone as identity; verify out-of-band.
BEC-RE-04

Opportune-moment monitoring

What it is
Travel, out-of-office or absence is awaited so requests go unchallenged.
Observable signals
A request timed to a known absence of the real person.
Defensive response
Delegation and verification norms that persist during absences.
BEC-EN

Engagement & Pretext

The request that sets the fraud in motion.

BEC-EN-01

Executive request

What it is
A supposed executive asks finance to act urgently and quietly.
Observable signals
An unusual executive payment request combining urgency and secrecy.
Defensive response
Mandatory independent verification for executive payment requests.
BEC-EN-02

Vendor bank-change request

What it is
A supposed supplier updates their payment details.
Observable signals
A banking-detail change requested over email.
Defensive response
Verify every change via a previously-known phone number.
BEC-EN-03

Fraudulent invoice submission

What it is
A fake or altered invoice is introduced into the process.
Observable signals
A new payee, altered account or mismatched totals.
Defensive response
Three-way match and payee allow-lists.
BEC-EN-04

Thread insertion

What it is
The request is placed inside a genuine ongoing conversation.
Observable signals
A banking change appearing mid a real thread.
Defensive response
Re-verify any change regardless of the thread it arrives in.
BEC-EN-05

Confidentiality framing

What it is
“Keep this between us” is used to block a second opinion.
Observable signals
Requests for secrecy around a payment.
Defensive response
Policy that no legitimate payment ever requires secrecy.
BEC-EX

Execution

How funds or data are actually moved.

BEC-EX-01

Wire-transfer instruction

What it is
A direct instruction to send funds to a specified account.
Observable signals
A first-time payee, urgency and a changed process.
Defensive response
Dual authorisation and callback verification for transfers.
BEC-EX-02

Banking-detail alteration

What it is
Account numbers on a real payment are changed.
Observable signals
Last-minute account changes before a payment run.
Defensive response
Verify all changes out-of-band; lock vendor bank data.
BEC-EX-03

Payroll diversion

What it is
An employee's direct-deposit destination is redirected.
Observable signals
Direct-deposit change requests arriving by email.
Defensive response
Authenticated self-service changes with confirmation.
BEC-EX-04

Gift-card request

What it is
Prepaid cards are requested as an untraceable payout.
Observable signals
An executive asking staff to buy gift cards.
Defensive response
Policy banning gift-card purchases requested by email.
BEC-EX-05

Verification suppression

What it is
Any second channel is discouraged or blocked.
Observable signals
Excuses for why a confirming phone call cannot happen.
Defensive response
Make out-of-band verification mandatory, never optional.
BEC-CO

Cash-out & Cover

How stolen funds are moved and the trail obscured.

BEC-CO-01

Money-mule routing

What it is
Funds pass through intermediary accounts to break the trail.
Observable signals
Payments to newly-added or unusual beneficiaries.
Defensive response
Beneficiary risk screening and rapid recall procedures.
BEC-CO-02

Rapid layering

What it is
Funds are moved quickly across accounts and borders.
Observable signals
Fast onward transfers immediately after receipt.
Defensive response
Real-time transaction monitoring and fast bank engagement.
BEC-CO-03

Evidence removal

What it is
Sent items, rules and traces are deleted on compromised accounts.
Observable signals
Missing sent mail or altered inbox rules.
Defensive response
Immutable logging and out-of-band forensics.
BEC-CO-04

Continued impersonation

What it is
Reassurance is used to stall discovery while funds clear.
Observable signals
Repeated claims that a payment is “processing”.
Defensive response
Independent confirmation with the real counterparty.
03.3 · Indicators & evidence model

No claim without an observable.

The BEC pillar shares the Email Abuse Intelligence evidence model — with payment-anomaly signals carrying particular weight.

CodeClassEvidence captured
EAI-EV-NETNetworkSending IP, ASN, reverse DNS and geolocation of the sender.
EAI-EV-AUTHAuthenticationSPF, DKIM and DMARC results and alignment for the claimed identity.
EAI-EV-CONTContentPayee and account details, invoice anomalies, tone and signature fingerprints.
EAI-EV-BEHAVBehaviouralLogin anomalies, new inbox rules, first-time-payee and out-of-pattern payment signals.
EAI-EV-INFRAInfrastructureDomain age, lookalike registration, hosting and registrar of any impersonating domain.
Confidence model
LowA single weak or ambiguous indicator. Suitable for enrichment, not enforcement.
ModerateMultiple independent indicators from different classes agree.
HighCorroborated indicators plus authentication or payment-anomaly evidence.
ConfirmedDirect, reproducible evidence — e.g. a verified fraudulent payee change.
