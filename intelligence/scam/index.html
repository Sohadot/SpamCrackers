Email Abuse Intelligence · Pillar SCM

Scam & Fraud.

The con that needs no code — advance-fee, romance, investment, prize, employment and extortion fraud all run on persuasion alone. This pillar models the con as a chain, from the first lure to the final cash-out. The fifth pillar of Email Abuse Intelligence.

Namespace EAI · SCM Release v1.0 Posture Defensive & analytical Techniques 25
01 · Pillar thesis

The exploit is the human, not the host.

Scams carry no payload a scanner can catch. There is nothing to detonate — only a story engineered to move a person from doubt to trust to payment. Because the vulnerability is psychological, the defence is pattern recognition: knowing the shapes these cons take, and the moment each one asks for money.

This pillar models the con as a repeatable chain — broadcast, hook, trust, extraction, escalation and cash-out — so the tactics can be named and taught. It bridges to the spam pillar for delivery at scale and to the BEC pillar where fraud turns on a business relationship rather than an individual.

02 · Scope & boundaries

Defensive by definition.

What this is
  • A classification and reference model for understanding email scams.
  • Analytical and defensive — for defenders, educators and responders.
  • Focused on recognisable patterns and protective guidance.
  • Open and citable, with stable identifiers.
What this is not
  • Not a script, template or playbook for running scams.
  • Not operational instructions for defrauding anyone.
  • Not a directory of victims or targets.
  • Not a substitute for lawful reporting and consumer protection.

Every technique is described at the level of classification and defence — what it is, how it is recognised, and how a potential victim is protected. The emphasis is protective throughout.

A con is described by its scam type, the emotional hook, the monetisation and how persistently it is pursued.

SCM-LB

Lure Broadcast

How the opening message is cast toward potential victims.

SCM-LB-01

Mass blast

What it is
A generic lure is sent at high volume to unfiltered recipients.
Observable signals
Identical or lightly-varied mail across many unrelated inboxes.
Defensive response
Bulk-pattern detection and sender reputation.
SCM-LB-02

Targeted list

What it is
A list tied to a theme (investors, jobseekers) is used for relevance.
Observable signals
Lures aligned to a specific audience or interest.
Defensive response
Context-aware filtering and user awareness for at-risk groups.
SCM-LB-03

Topical & seasonal

What it is
Current events, holidays or disasters are used to raise plausibility.
Observable signals
A lure closely tracking a news event or season.
Defensive response
Heightened scrutiny of event-themed solicitations.
SCM-LB-04

Authority / brand veneer

What it is
A trusted institution or brand is invoked to open the door.
Observable signals
Institutional branding paired with an unofficial sender.
Defensive response
Brand-to-sender consistency checks.
SCM-HK

Hook & Pretext

The story that captures attention and intent.

SCM-HK-01

Windfall claim

What it is
An unexpected prize, inheritance or refund is dangled.
Observable signals
Notification of winnings the recipient never entered.
Defensive response
Treat unsolicited windfalls as fraud by default.
SCM-HK-02

Romance opener

What it is
A friendly or affectionate first contact begins a relationship con.
Observable signals
Unsolicited personal approaches steering off-platform.
Defensive response
Awareness of romance-fraud patterns; verify identities.
SCM-HK-03

Investment pitch

What it is
A high-return scheme or platform is offered.
Observable signals
Guaranteed or outsized returns with urgency.
Defensive response
Independent verification of any investment solicitation.
SCM-HK-04

Job / task offer

What it is
An easy, well-paid role is offered, often as a mule funnel.
Observable signals
Unsolicited offers involving receiving or forwarding money.
Defensive response
Verify employers; flag money-handling 'jobs'.
SCM-HK-05

Threat / extortion

What it is
Fear is used — claimed compromise, debt or legal action.
Observable signals
Payment demands under threat, often to crypto.
Defensive response
Recognise extortion templates; never pay, report.
SCM-TR

Trust Building

How credibility is manufactured before the ask.

SCM-TR-01

Channel migration

What it is
The victim is moved to a private channel away from oversight.
Observable signals
Early pressure to move to chat or personal email.
Defensive response
Awareness that off-platform moves reduce protection.
SCM-TR-02

Fabricated proof

What it is
Fake documents, screenshots or credentials are shown.
Observable signals
Unverifiable 'evidence' supporting the story.
Defensive response
Independently verify any document or claim.
SCM-TR-03

Small reciprocity

What it is
A tiny gift or early 'return' builds false confidence.
Observable signals
A small payout preceding a larger request.
Defensive response
Recognise reciprocity as a manipulation lever.
SCM-TR-04

Urgency & scarcity

What it is
A closing window pressures fast, unconsidered action.
Observable signals
Deadlines attached to money movement.
Defensive response
Slow down; legitimate opportunities survive verification.
SCM-EX

Extraction

How money or data is actually taken.

SCM-EX-01

Advance-fee demand

What it is
Fees or taxes are required before the promised payout.
Observable signals
Payment demanded to release a larger sum.
Defensive response
No legitimate payout requires an upfront fee.
SCM-EX-02

Payment-detail capture

What it is
Bank or card details are solicited directly.
Observable signals
Requests for full card or banking data by email.
Defensive response
Never share payment details via unsolicited mail.
SCM-EX-03

Crypto transfer

What it is
Irreversible cryptocurrency payment is requested.
Observable signals
Wallet addresses and urgency to transfer.
Defensive response
Treat crypto demands as a strong fraud indicator.
SCM-EX-04

Gift-card demand

What it is
Prepaid card codes are requested as payment.
Observable signals
Requests to buy cards and share codes.
Defensive response
No legitimate body is paid in gift cards.
SCM-EX-05

Data harvest

What it is
Identity or account data is collected for later fraud.
Observable signals
Requests for identity documents or credentials.
Defensive response
Minimise data sharing; verify the requester.
SCM-ES

Escalation & Reload

How losses are compounded after the first payment.

SCM-ES-01

Rising fees

What it is
New obstacles justify ever-larger payments.
Observable signals
A sequence of growing 'final' fees.
Defensive response
Recognise the sunk-cost trap; stop and report.
SCM-ES-02

Sunk-cost pressure

What it is
Prior losses are used to push further payment.
Observable signals
Appeals to protect what was already paid.
Defensive response
Past loss never justifies new payment.
SCM-ES-03

Reload targeting

What it is
A prior victim is re-approached, sometimes as a fake 'recovery' service.
Observable signals
Follow-up offers to recover lost funds — for a fee.
Defensive response
Treat unsolicited recovery offers as second-stage fraud.
SCM-ES-04

Mule recruitment

What it is
The victim is enlisted to move others' stolen funds.
Observable signals
Requests to receive and forward money.
Defensive response
Awareness that money-handling requests enable laundering.
SCM-CO

Cash-out & Evasion

How proceeds are collected and traced away.

SCM-CO-01

Mule networks

What it is
Funds pass through recruited intermediaries.
Observable signals
Payments routed via unrelated personal accounts.
Defensive response
Beneficiary screening and rapid recall procedures.
SCM-CO-02

Crypto laundering

What it is
Cryptocurrency is layered to obscure the trail.
Observable signals
Rapid movement across wallets and mixers.
Defensive response
On-chain analytics and fast reporting.
SCM-CO-03

Identity churn

What it is
Personas, addresses and accounts are cycled to evade blocks.
Observable signals
Recurring scripts from ever-changing identities.
Defensive response
Cluster campaigns by template and behaviour, not identity.
03.3 · Indicators & evidence model

No claim without an observable.

The scam pillar shares the Email Abuse Intelligence evidence model — with narrative and payment-request signals carrying particular weight.

CodeClassEvidence captured
EAI-EV-NETNetworkSending IP, ASN, reverse DNS and geolocation.
EAI-EV-AUTHAuthenticationSPF, DKIM and DMARC results for the sending identity.
EAI-EV-CONTContentLure templates, wallet and account details, narrative fingerprints, reply-to patterns.
EAI-EV-BEHAVBehaviouralChannel migration, escalation sequences, payment-request patterns.
EAI-EV-INFRAInfrastructureDomain age, disposable-mail use, wallet and mule-account reuse.
Confidence model
LowA single weak or ambiguous indicator. Suitable for enrichment, not enforcement.
ModerateMultiple independent indicators from different classes agree.
HighCorroborated indicators plus payment or narrative evidence.
ConfirmedDirect, reproducible evidence — e.g. a documented payment demand and wallet.
