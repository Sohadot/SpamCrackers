06 · Field guide

The questions behind the signal.

Plain answers to what SPF, DKIM and DMARC really do — and what your grade means.

What do SPF, DKIM and DMARC actually do?
SPF lists which servers may send mail as your domain, DKIM adds a cryptographic signature receivers can verify, and DMARC tells receivers what to do when a message fails those checks. Together they stop spammers from impersonating your domain. Check yours →
What is a good email security score?
Under the SpamCrackers Standard, an A (85 or above) means your domain is armored: a valid SPF hard-fail policy, a published DKIM key, and an enforced DMARC policy. Anything graded C or below leaves gaps a spoofer can exploit.
Does SpamCrackers store my domain or email?
No. Every check runs entirely in your browser against public DNS. Nothing you enter is sent to a server, logged, or stored — there is no server in the loop. See the Standard for how this works.
How do I fix a low grade?
Publish an SPF record ending in -all, enable DKIM signing in your mail provider, and add a DMARC record — starting at p=none to monitor, then raising it to quarantine and reject. Each result lists the exact fix for every gap.
Is SpamCrackers free?
Yes. The domain and message checkers are completely free and require no signup.
