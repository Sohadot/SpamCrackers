The SpamCrackers Standard · v1.0

One grade for email trust.

Email authentication is four separate records and a lot of jargon. The Standard turns them into a single, transparent grade — A+ to F — for whether a domain is armored against impersonation. Public rubric, computed live in your browser, versioned and citable.

01 · Why a standard

Posture you can read at a glance.

Ask most tools whether your email is secure and you get four raw verdicts and a shrug. Is a soft-fail SPF with monitoring-only DMARC good or bad? Compared to what? Raw pass/fail hides the answer that actually matters: how exposed am I, and to whom?

The Standard answers that in one symbol. It weighs the four controls that determine whether someone can send mail as you, rolls them into a score, and maps that score to a grade with a plain meaning — from Sovereign to Open. The rubric is public and fixed, so two domains graded a year apart are graded the same way.

02 · What it grades

Four controls. Twenty-five points each.

The rubric · 100 points
SPF Only servers you authorise may send as your domain.
Without it, anyone can send mail as you and no record contradicts them. SPF is the foundation of sender identity.
 +25
DKIM Every message carries a signature receivers can verify.
A cryptographic proof that the message is genuinely yours and unaltered — impossible to forge without your private key.
 +25
DMARC Receivers are told to reject anything that fails.
Ties SPF and DKIM to the visible From address and enforces a policy. The only control that reliably stops exact-domain spoofing.
 +25
MX Mail is received and can be filtered.
No MX means no inbound mail — and nowhere to apply inbound protection. It anchors the receiving side of the domain.
 +25
The grade scale
A+ 95–100
SovereignThe reference standard — fully armored across all four controls.
A 85–94
ArmoredProtected against impersonation, with strong enforcement.
B 70–84
GuardedSolid, with room to harden one control further.
C 50–69
PartialReal gaps a spoofer can exploit today.
D 30–49
ExposedWeak controls — the domain is easily spoofed.
F 0–29
OpenWide open to impersonation. No meaningful protection.
03 · Methodology

Transparent, deterministic, private.

Live & in-browser

Every check runs in your browser over DNS-over-HTTPS against public DNS. No domain, result or email reaches a server — there is none in the loop — and nothing is stored.

Deterministic

The same records always produce the same grade. The rubric and thresholds are published here and fixed within a version.

Transparent

Each result shows the record it read and the exact fix for every gap, mapped to the Remediation Playbook.

One honest caveat

DKIM is detected against a set of common selectors. A domain using a custom selector may show DKIM as not found even when it is present — the single heuristic in an otherwise exact grade. SPF, DMARC and MX are read directly.

04 · Version & citation

Fixed, versioned, citable.

The Standard is v1.0. Changes to the rubric or thresholds are versioned transparently under model governance.
